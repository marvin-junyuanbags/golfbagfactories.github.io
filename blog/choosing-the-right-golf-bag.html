Choosing the Right Golf Bag: Stand, Cart, or Tour?

Published on January 31, 2025 | By Golf Gear Gurus

Selecting the right golf bag is a critical decision that impacts your comfort and convenience on the course. The three main categories—stand bags, cart bags, and tour bags—each serve a different purpose. Understanding their features, benefits, and drawbacks will help you find the perfect match for your game.

Stand Bags: For the Walker

Stand bags are designed for golfers who prefer to walk the course. Their defining feature is the retractable leg mechanism that allows the bag to stand on its own on uneven terrain.

Pros:

  • Lightweight: Typically the lightest option, making them easy to carry for 18 holes.
  • Versatile: Can be carried or used on a push cart or golf cart.
  • Convenient: The stand mechanism keeps the bag upright and clubs accessible.

Cons:

  • Less Storage: Fewer pockets and less overall storage space than cart or tour bags.
  • Smaller Dividers: Club dividers are often smaller, which can lead to some club crowding.

Cart Bags: For the Rider

As the name suggests, cart bags are designed to be used on a golf cart or push cart. They are larger and offer more features than stand bags.

Pros:

  • Ample Storage: Numerous, spacious pockets for apparel, valuables, and drinks.
  • Larger Dividers: Often feature 14- or 15-way full-length dividers to keep clubs organized and protected.
  • Easy Access: Pockets are typically forward-facing for easy access when the bag is on a cart.

Cons:

  • Heavy and Bulky: Not practical for carrying.
  • No Stand: A flat, non-slip base is designed for carts, not for standing on the ground.

Tour Bags (Staff Bags): For the Professional

Tour bags, also known as staff bags, are what you see the pros use. They are the largest, heaviest, and most premium option, offering maximum storage and durability.

Pros:

  • Maximum Storage: Huge pockets for everything a professional golfer might need.
  • Premium Materials: Constructed from high-end materials like leather for a luxurious look and feel.
  • Ultimate Club Protection: Generous padding and large, well-structured dividers.

Cons:

  • Extremely Heavy: The heaviest type of bag, designed to be carried by a caddie.
  • Expensive: The most costly option on the market.
  • Impractical for Most Amateurs: Overkill for the average golfer in terms of size and weight.

Which One is Right for You?

  • If you primarily walk the course, a Stand Bag is your best bet.
  • If you almost always use a golf cart or push cart, a Cart Bag offers superior organization and storage.
  • If you want the professional look and have a caddie (or just want the best of the best), a Tour Bag is the ultimate choice.
